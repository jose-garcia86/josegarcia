# Evaluate Phase

After you have deployed your system, you will move into the evaluation phase of your project. In this phase, you’ll attempt to measure how successful your project was or continues to be and communicate results to all the relevant stakeholders.

Measure the impact of your project can be tricky, but if you’ve been following this framewor, then all the way back in explore phase, you should have defined what success looks like, at least qualitatively, or in a detailed and specific problem statement.

For any AI project, the technical component of the AI is just part of the solution. It’s one component of a much larger and potentially complicated technology or product. And so the performance of the AI model in itself may not be the most imporntat aspect of a successful outcome, and certainly not the only one.

A sort of system where humans are involved in manual labeling and annotating data is not uncommon in AI applications. Maybe even the majority of AI systems are systems where there have been mindful about continually getting human labels in order to update the models. And most of those could be with experts. This is why its important to emphasize that ensuring a good user experience for anootators, is as important as complicated as designing the pure machine learning parts of any AI solution.  

A lot of approaches like this, which sound good initially, tend not to actually work so well. There’s a lot of evidence that if you try to gamify a system, that becomes a sort of fatigue in itself, and the only people who have a better experience in their work going forward are the people at the very top of whatever kind of leaderboard and gamification scoring system.

So it’s very hard to think about what is the right way that you need to create an interface where you can combine human and machine intelligence.
